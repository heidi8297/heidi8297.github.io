Takeaways from the data (contains spoilers)

  • Harry steals the show with over half of the total spells coming from him.
  • It's easy to spot some of the more pivotal moments in the books where the spells are clustered together.
  • There's definitely more spell activity in book 7, but it's more spread out than in the other books. As a fan, this reflects my memories of reading them.
  • Poor Ron doesn't get half as many spells as his lady Ms. Granger and is even bested by his own mother, poor thing (we'll throw him a bone and point out that the scope of this data is limited to the top 10 spells).
  • The evil/aggressive spells come almost entirely from the Death Eaters (surprising no one), with one noteworthy exception: Severus Snape twice performs the "Avada Kedavra" (killing) curse, but he was of course acting as a spy and only doing so when necessary.
  • None of the top 10 spells appeared in the first book. One of them (the Muffliato spell) doesn't debut until book 6.
